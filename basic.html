Introduction to the basics of Guitar

Welcome to the guitar basics tutorial! Here, you will be introduced to the fundamentals of playing the guitar, including proper posture, chord formation, and strumming techniques. Whether you are a complete beginner or just looking to brush up on your skills, this tutorial is for you. Let's get started on your musical journey!

First Chords


Chords are the backbone for all music. After mastering a few chords, you'll be able to learn to play a few songs. There are two main type of chords that form the core of most popular music, major and minor chords. Major chords start with the chord letter capatalized whilst minor chords have and 'm' attached to it. Major chords have a bright, strong sound whilt minor chords have a mellow, sobre sound.

Simple Chord Sequences


A chord sequence is a series of chords played in a specific order. To play a major chord on the guitar, you'll need to play the root note, as well as the third and fifth notes of the major scale. To play a minor chord, you'll need to play the root note, the third note of the minor scale, and the fifth note of the major scale. Here are some examples of simple chord sequences that you can use to play a variety of different styles of music:

  • The 4-chord sequence: This is commonly used in pop and rock music and consists of four chords arranged in the pattern I-IV-V-IV.
  • The 12-bar blues: This is commonly used in blues music and consists of 12 bars of music with three chords per bar, arranged in the pattern I-IV-I-I-IV-IV-I-I-V-IV-I-I.
  • The 2-5-1 sequence: This is commonly used in jazz music and consists of three chords played in the pattern II-V-I.
  • The 3-chord sequence: This is very simple and consists of only three chords. It is often used in folk and country music and can be played in a variety of different ways, such as the pattern I-IV-V.

With practice and experimentation, you'll be able to come up with your own chord sequences and play a wide range of songs on the guitar.

Strumming


Strumming is the act of playing a series of notes on the guitar by striking the strings with the pick. There are four main types of strumming patterns that you can use to play songs on the guitar:

  • Downstroke: A downstroke is when you strum the strings from the bottom to the top of the guitar. This is the most common strumming pattern and is used in most songs.
  • Upstroke: An upstroke is when you strum the strings from the top to the bottom of the guitar. This is a less common strumming pattern and is used in some songs.
  • Down-up strumming: Down-up strumming is when you strum the strings from the bottom to the top of the guitar, then from the top to the bottom of the guitar. This is a less common strumming pattern and is used in some songs.
  • Down-up-down strumming: Down-up-down strumming is when you strum the strings from the bottom to the top of the guitar, then from the top to the bottom of the guitar, then from the bottom to the top of the guitar. This is a less common strumming pattern and is used in some songs.

Finger-Picking


Finger-picking is the act of playing a series of notes on the guitar by plucking the strings with your fingers. There are four main types of finger-picking patterns that you can use to play songs on the guitar:

  • Single-note finger-picking: Single-note finger-picking is when you pluck each string individually. This is the most common finger-picking pattern and is used in most songs.
  • Double-note finger-picking: Double-note finger-picking is when you pluck two strings at the same time. This is a less common finger-picking pattern and is used in some songs.
  • Triple-note finger-picking: Triple-note finger-picking is when you pluck three strings at the same time. This is a less common finger-picking pattern and is used in some songs.
  • Quadruple-note finger-picking: Quadruple-note finger-picking is when you pluck four strings at the same time. This is a less common finger-picking pattern and is used in some songs.
