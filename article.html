The most popular playing style right now!

One of the most exciting developments in the guitar world in recent years has been the rise of the fingerstyle guitar technique. Fingerstyle guitar is a technique where the fingers are used to pluck the strings rather than a pick. This technique allows for a more delicate and nuanced sound, and has been used by guitarists in a wide range of genres, from folk and classical music to jazz and pop.
Fingerstyle guitarists like Andy McKee and Antoine Dufour have become popular on social media platforms like YouTube and Instagram, and have inspired many players to pick up the technique. The fingerstyle technique allows for a more expressive and emotional sound, and allows for greater versatility in playing. It can be used for a wide range of styles, from delicate fingerpicking to percussive and rhythmic playing.
Fingerstyle guitar also allows for the use of multiple techniques such as harmonics, harmonics slaps, and tapping which makes it unique and adds depth to the music. This technique also enables the guitarist to play melody and accompaniment at the same time, which creates a rich, full-sounding experience.
The rise of fingerstyle guitar has also led to an increase in the popularity of fingerstyle guitar competitions and festivals. These events bring together fingerstyle guitarists from all over the world and showcase the incredible talent and diversity within the fingerstyle guitar community.
In conclusion, the rise of fingerstyle guitar has been one of the most exciting developments in the guitar world. This technique allows for a more delicate and nuanced sound, and has been used by guitarists in a wide range of genres. Fingerstyle guitarists like Andy McKee and Antoine Dufour have become popular on social media platforms and have inspired many players to pick up the technique. The rise of fingerstyle guitar has also led to an increase in the popularity of fingerstyle guitar competitions and festivals and it's great to see this unique technique being celebrated.
