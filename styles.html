What style of music are you interested in?

Musical Style Information
Reggae A jamaican style of music with four beats to the measure and the off-beats strongly accented.
Flamenco Flamenco is a style of music and dance that originated in southern Spain and is characterized by its fast, rhythmic strumming and percussive guitar playing.
Classical Classical music on guitar is typically played with a combination of arpeggios, melodies, and chordal accompaniment, using precise fingerpicking techniques and a fluid, expressive style.
Country Country music is a genre of popular music that originated in the southern United States in the early 1920s. It takes its roots from the southeastern genre of American folk music and Western music.
Folk Folk music includes both traditional music and the genre that evolved from it during the 20th century folk revival. The term originated in the 19th century, but is often applied to music older than that.
Soul & Funk Soul music is a popular music genre that originated in the United States in the late 1950s and early 1960s. It combines elements of African-American gospel music, rhythm and blues, and jazz.
Latin Latin music is a genre of music that originated in the 1950s and 1960s, and is a fusion of Latin American music with American popular music. It includes a wide variety of styles, including salsa, merengue, cumbia, bolero, mambo, samba, bossa nova, and many others.
African African music is the music of the African continent, including the music of the African diaspora. African music is divided into many subgenres, some of which are widely popular worldwide.
Blues Blues is a genre and musical form which was originated in the Deep South of the United States around the end of the 19th century by African Americans from roots in African musical traditions, African-American work songs, spirituals, and the folk music of white Americans of European heritage.
Rock Rock music is a genre of popular music that originated as "rock and roll" in the United States in the early 1950s, and developed into a range of different styles in the 1960s and later, particularly in the United States and the United Kingdom.
Jazz Jazz is a music genre that originated in the African-American communities of New Orleans, United States, in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, and developed from roots in blues and ragtime. Jazz is seen by many as "America's classical music".
Metal Metal music is a genre of rock music that developed in the late 1960s and early 1970s, largely in the United Kingdom and the United States. It is characterized by heavy use of aggressive vocals, distorted electric guitars, bass guitar, drums, keyboard instruments and often accompanied with pianos and strings.
